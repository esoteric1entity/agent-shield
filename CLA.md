# Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

The Agent Architect Stack project — of which agent-shield is the security
component — uses a CLA (Contributor License Agreement) to
ensure that contributions can be incorporated into the project under the project's
license AND that the project retains the option to consider future license changes
with proper contributor consent.

This is a one-time agreement you sign before your first pull request can be merged.
Subsequent PRs do not require re-signing.

## Why we need a CLA

When you submit a contribution, you retain copyright on your code (good — that's
yours forever). The CLA is a **license grant** from you to the project: you give
the project permission to use your contribution under:

1. **The project's current license** (Apache 2.0), so it can be distributed
   alongside the rest of the project; AND
2. **Any future license the project may adopt**, subject to a contributor
   vote process described below.

Without #2, the project would need to track down every contributor ever to ask
permission for any future license change (e.g., moving to BSL for commercial
reasons). With #2, the project can move forward while still respecting your
rights.

This is the **same mechanism** used by the Apache Foundation (ICLA), Google
(Google CLA), Microsoft, Facebook/Meta, and most large open-source projects.

## What you sign

A short agreement (~1 page) covering:

1. **You own the contribution** or have the right to submit it.
2. **You grant the project a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, no-charge,
   royalty-free, irrevocable copyright license** to use the contribution under
   the project's current license (Apache 2.0).
3. **You grant the project a license to use the contribution under any future
   license** the project adopts, **provided** the project conducts a
   contributor vote and a majority of contributors (by commit count over the
   last 12 months) approve the relicense.
4. **You grant the project a patent license** consistent with Apache 2.0.
5. **You warrant** that the contribution does not knowingly include any
   third-party code under incompatible licenses.
6. **You understand** that you retain copyright on your contribution and may
   use it however you wish in other projects.

## The contributor-vote safeguard (re: point 3)

A future relicense **cannot happen** by simple project-owner decision. It
requires:

1. A 30-day public discussion period on the issue tracker.
2. A formal vote of all contributors with commits in the last 12 months.
3. A two-thirds majority of vote-weight (by commit count) to approve.
4. The project owner to formally execute the relicense.

If at any point a contributor's contribution is being relicensed and that
contributor objects, the contribution can be **removed** from the relicense
and the project will either (a) relicense the remaining code under a compatible
license, or (b) fork the contribution and reach out to the contributor for a
specific agreement. This is a last-resort safeguard.

## How to sign

When you submit your first pull request, a CLA bot will check whether you've
signed. If you haven't, the bot will guide you through a one-time signature
flow on a third-party CLA service (e.g., CLA Assistant, EasyCLA, or
Contributor Agreements). The signature is bound to your GitHub account and
persists across all future PRs.

The signature takes about 2 minutes and does not require a lawyer.

## What you DO NOT sign

- You do NOT transfer copyright. You retain full copyright on your work.
- You do NOT give up the right to use your own code in other projects.
- You do NOT grant the project any rights to your *future* work that you don't
  submit.
- You do NOT sign away your right to be credited. The project's attribution
  practices (see [`AUTHORS.md`](./AUTHORS.md)) continue to apply.

## For corporate contributors

If you are contributing on behalf of an employer, your employer may also need
to sign a Corporate CLA (CCLA) before the contribution can be accepted. This
is standard for corporate contributors and protects both you and your
employer. The CLA bot will guide you through this if it detects an employer
email domain in your commits.
